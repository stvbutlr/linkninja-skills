# ICP Archetype Templates

Starting-point ICP templates for the most common LinkNinja customer archetypes. Use these as scaffolding — the goal isn't to copy-paste but to refine each line with the user's actual language. Marketing-speak is forgettable; the prospect's actual phrasing is what makes drafts land.

Each template covers all 6 ICP dimensions (role, life stage, daily frustrations, aspirations, language, cultural context).

---

## Archetype 1 — Solo Consultant

**Role / Industry / Business model**

Independent consultant, 1-3 years post-corporate or 5+ years running their own practice. Solo or with one ops support person. Typical niches: recruitment, IT/digital transformation, business strategy, marketing, operations, HR/people, sustainability/ESG. Annual revenue $100K–$1M, mostly retainers and project work.

**Life stage / transitions**

Past the "is this real?" phase, now in the "how do I make this consistent?" phase. Often just hired their first ops support, or about to. Has 2-4 anchor clients but pipeline goes feast-or-famine. Looking at year 2-3 with the question: do I scale this into a firm, or stay solo and raise rates?

**Daily frustrations** (their words, not yours)

- "Lead flow is the thing that keeps me up at night."
- "Half my discovery calls go nowhere and I can't tell what predicts who'll convert."
- "I spend more time pitching than doing the actual work."
- "Referrals are great but unpredictable — I want a system."
- "I don't have time for content but I know I need it."

**Aspirations**

- Predictable monthly inbound (pipeline, not luck)
- Higher-ticket clients without working harder
- Either: scale to a 5-10 person firm OR stay solo at $300K+ doing work they love
- Become a known voice in their niche

**Language / jargon**

- Internal: "discovery call," "scope creep," "engagement length," "retainer," "project rate," "deliverable," "stakeholder"
- Industry-specific: depends on niche — recruitment uses "placement," "fee structure"; ops uses "process map," "throughput"
- Cultural: "feast or famine," "the dream client problem," "year 3 wall"

**Cultural context**

Mostly Western markets (US, UK, Australia, Canada). Casual-professional in DMs but formal in proposals. Values directness over polish. Will reject anything that smells like agency-style "we'll partner with you on your transformation journey." Wants to talk to a peer, not be sold to.

## Archetype 2 — Coach (Executive / Business / Career / Performance)

**Role / Industry / Business model**

1:1 coach or cohort-based coach. Often left a previous career (sales, leadership, HR, therapy) to coach. Charges $200–$2,000/session for 1:1 or $2K–$20K for cohort/program. Typical niches: executive, career, leadership, sales, business/founder, performance, health/wellness.

**Life stage / transitions**

Year 2-3 in the practice. Has signature offer (1:1 program, group cohort, or hybrid). Past first launch and iterating. Just opened a second cohort, or contemplating one. Sometimes considering hiring a coach trainer or junior coaches.

**Daily frustrations**

- "Cohort fill is stressful — feast or famine."
- "I keep getting on discovery calls with people who can't actually afford the program."
- "My audience is small but engaged — how do I scale without it feeling like a pyramid?"
- "I'm tired of the 'inspirational quote' content treadmill but it's what gets engagement."
- "My ICP is broad and I know that's why marketing feels hard."

**Aspirations**

- Cohorts that fill themselves through reputation
- A waitlist
- Higher-ticket 1:1 work without being on Zoom 8 hours a day
- Authority in their niche — known voice, podcast guest, book deal
- Eventually a small team / certification program

**Language / jargon**

- Internal: "cohort," "container," "session," "intake," "completion," "transformation," "the work"
- Industry-specific: "presence," "embodiment," "blocker," "next-level"
- Anti-vocabulary: avoid corporate-speak ("optimize," "scale"), avoid hard-sell language

**Cultural context**

Primarily English-speaking markets. Casual, warm tone in DMs. Values authenticity over expertise. Will react against anything that feels transactional. The "talking to a mate" voice resonates strongly. Values are often explicit (mission, purpose, alignment).

## Archetype 3 — Independent Financial Advisor / Wealth Manager

**Role / Industry / Business model**

Independent (RIA in US, IFA in UK, similar in AU/CA), or working at a small/boutique firm with autonomy over their book. Book value $20M–$300M AUM. Charges fee-based (typically 0.5%–1.25% AUM) or flat-fee planning. Sometimes adds tax/estate/succession planning.

**Life stage / transitions**

5+ years independent. Established book. Now thinking about: growing the book by 30-50%, succession planning, transitioning to fee-only, or specialising in a niche (medical professionals, founders post-exit, divorced women, etc.).

**Daily frustrations**

- "Compliance review takes weeks and slows everything down."
- "Most of my new clients come from referrals — but referrals are slowing as my book ages."
- "I want to add younger clients but my brand is read as old-school."
- "LinkedIn feels stiff. I post quarterly and nothing happens."
- "The advisors who 'do content' get clients — I can see it. I just can't get started."

**Aspirations**

- A consistent inbound flow that doesn't depend on referrals
- Add 15-30 net new clients per year at the right asset level
- Build a personal brand that survives a firm transition
- Potentially specialise (succession planning for founders, women's wealth, etc.)
- Leave the firm and go fully independent — or grow the team

**Language / jargon**

- Internal: "AUM," "fact-find," "discovery meeting," "fiduciary," "fee-based," "planning engagement," "book of business"
- Industry-specific: "rollover," "RMD," "tax-loss harvesting," "succession," "estate plan," "concentrated position"
- Cultural: "the right fit," "the relationship," "long-term thinking"

**Cultural context**

Highly compliance-aware — anything in writing has to pass review. Tone leans formal by default. Values trust and longevity over flash. Will react against anything that smells like a sales pitch. The challenge: warm them up enough that the "talking to a mate" voice feels appropriate. Once trusted, very loyal.

## Archetype 4 — Agency Owner

**Role / Industry / Business model**

Founder-led agency, 5-30 staff. Niches: SEO, creative/branding, content, paid media, web development, LinkedIn/social media. Annual revenue $500K–$5M. Sells retainers ($3K–$30K/month) or project work ($10K–$200K).

**Life stage / transitions**

Past the "is this real?" phase. Has a delivery team. Now wrestling with: founder-led sales (founder is the salesperson, capacity bottleneck), pricing power (can we charge premium?), specialisation (broaden vs niche down), and team scaling (next 3 hires).

**Daily frustrations**

- "Founder-led sales is a bottleneck. I can't keep being the only one bringing in business."
- "We do great work but can't charge premium prices."
- "Project clients are more profitable but retainer clients are more stable — I want both, can't have both."
- "Our case studies are buried in our portfolio. Nobody reads them."
- "Outbound feels gross but referrals alone aren't enough."

**Aspirations**

- A second salesperson (or a fractional one) who can close 60% of what the founder closes
- Premium pricing that reflects the actual value
- A niche reputation — known for X, not "we do digital marketing"
- Bigger contracts (3-year retainers, multi-engagement clients)
- Potentially: exit / acquisition

**Language / jargon**

- Internal: "scope," "retainer," "discovery," "kickoff," "creative brief," "pitch deck," "case study," "SOW"
- Industry-specific: depends on niche — SEO uses "ranking," "traffic," "domain authority"; creative uses "brand system," "campaign," "creative direction"
- Cultural: "founder-led sales," "scope creep," "overdelivery problem"

**Cultural context**

Casual-professional in their own voice but their agency marketing tends to be polished/corporate. Goal: get them writing in their founder voice, not their agency-website voice. Values craft, hates "agency-speak" (synergy, partnership, bespoke). Aussie/UK/US/Canadian markets dominate.

## Archetype 5 — Niche Service Seller

**Role / Industry / Business model**

Highly varied — this archetype is a catch-all. Examples:

- Specialist accountant (real estate, e-commerce, content creators)
- Fractional executive (CFO, CMO, COO)
- Copywriter / specialist freelancer
- Course creator / educator
- Therapist / counsellor
- Author / speaker / thought leader
- Specialist (fitness, nutrition, fertility, divorce, immigration)

Revenue ranges from $50K (early therapist) to $2M+ (established fractional CFO with multiple long-term retainers). Sells retainers, packages, or hourly.

**Life stage / transitions**

Wherever they are. The defining feature is **niche specificity**: their offer is tied to a clear customer profile and a clear outcome. They've moved past "I help anyone with X" to "I help [specific person] with [specific outcome]."

**Daily frustrations** (varies by sub-niche, but common patterns)

- "My niche is so specific that mass marketing doesn't work — I need to find each prospect by hand."
- "LinkedIn is where my buyers are but I don't know how to reach them without being weird."
- "Referrals dry up because my niche is small."
- "Generic sales advice doesn't apply to my world."

**Aspirations**

- Consistent flow of right-fit clients
- Pricing power (premium positioning in the niche)
- Becoming THE name in their niche
- Building authority through content / speaking / a book

**Language / jargon**

- **Highly niche-specific.** Don't assume — ask. The whole point of this archetype is that their language is distinctive and matches their customers' language exactly.

**Cultural context**

Niche-specific. The user should describe their cultural context themselves rather than picking a default.

## How to Use These Templates

1. After the archetype detection in `onboarding-walkthrough` Step 0, surface the matching template here.
2. Read it to the user as a **starting point**, then refine each section with their actual specifics:
   - Their exact language for daily frustrations (quote real client phrases)
   - Their actual aspiration framing (not the generic version)
   - Their cultural context (geography, formality, distinctive style)
3. Save the refined ICP via `update_context(additional_context="...")`.
4. Hand off to **voice-profile-setup**, then **stage-configuration** — the archetype shapes those too.

If the user's business doesn't fit any archetype above, build their ICP from scratch using the 6-dimension interview in `icp-definition`. Then consider contributing the new archetype back via `CONTRIBUTING.md`.

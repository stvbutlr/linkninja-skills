# Sell-By-Chat Methodology

The principles every DM skill in this collection embeds. Sourced from [Steve Butler's *Sell By Chat Playbook*](https://library.sevenfigurecreators.com/3/the-sell-by-chat-playbook).

> **Stop selling. Start serving.**
>
> Relationship-first, transaction-second. People hate being sold to but love being helped. The sale is a side-effect of genuine helpfulness, not the objective.

## The Conversion Funnel

How a chat becomes a contract. Use these benchmarks in `pipeline-health-check`:

| Stage transition | Healthy rate |
|---|---|
| Chats started → replies | ~30% |
| Replies → engagement | ~50% |
| Engagement → call booked | ~20% |
| Call booked → showed up | ~80% |
| Showed up → closed | ~50% |
| **Overall: chats → contracts** | **~1%** |

If a stage falls below half its benchmark, that's the leaky stage to fix first.

## Opening Messages

Three rules for every cold DM and post-event opener:

1. **Make it about them.** Not your offer, your company, or your method.
2. **Keep it short.** 2–4 sentences. The weight per word goes up as length goes down.
3. **Serve, don't take.** Lead with an observation, question, or helpful comment — not an ask.

### Precision Flattery

Specific niche praise that proves you understand their world. *"Your approach to [specific topic] is sharp because [specific reason]."* Generic praise ("love your content") signals automation. Pull from `enrich_connections` data — recent posts, experience, projects — to make praise specific and credible (*requires an active Sales Navigator connection*; without Sales Nav, fall back to headline-level cues).

### Pattern Interrupt

Unexpected but relevant observation. Not random humour — a relatable insight tied to their situation. The point: prove you've stepped into their world before opening your mouth.

### Preloaded Value

If you offer something, it should be:

- **Specific** — tied to their situation, not generic
- **Ungated** — no email opt-in, no scheduling step
- **Actionable** — a checklist, framework, or single insight they can use today
- **Tied to next conversation** — opens a loop, doesn't close one

### Anti-patterns

- "Just checking in" / "I hope this finds you well"
- Generic openers that could go to anyone
- Pitching anything in the first message
- "I'd love to hop on a quick call"

## Replies & Qualifying

### A–B Method

Find their **Point A** (current state, current pain) and their **Point B** (desired state, what they want). The gap between them is your opportunity. Every question you ask should help surface A or B more clearly — never to set up a pitch.

### Question Sequence

Three steps, in order. One question per message:

1. **Easy open.** Broad, low-pressure. *"What's been your biggest focus this quarter?"*
2. **Go deeper.** Into the specific challenge they hinted at. *"When you mentioned X — is that more of a Y issue or a Z issue?"*
3. **Test commitment.** A micro-commitment that surfaces readiness. *"Is fixing this something you're actively working on?"*

Don't stack questions. Wait for an answer before asking the next one.

### Trust Progression

Each reply moves the prospect through internal checkpoints:

- *"Is this real?"* → reply naturally, match their energy
- *"Is this for me?"* → ask questions specific to their world
- *"Do you get my world?"* → demonstrate insider knowledge
- *"Is it safe?"* → give value before asking for anything

One step per interaction. Don't try to skip checkpoints.

## Handling Objections

Pattern: **Acknowledge → Ask Context → Reframe.**

1. **Acknowledge.** Validate the concern fully. *"That makes sense — timing is tricky."*
2. **Ask context.** What's behind the stated objection? *"What would need to change for this to feel right?"*
3. **Reframe.** Position the solution against the underlying concern, with specifics from their world.

### Never use "but"

"But" negates everything before it. They'll hear the negation, not the acknowledgment. Use "and" or a paragraph break instead.

### Make it safer, not louder

The bigger the objection, the more they need to feel safe — not more persuaded. Reduce risk: smaller next step, easy out, no pressure.

## Booking Calls

### Micro-commitments

Build emotional investment through small steps. The call invite is a micro-commitment, not a big ask.

### The 3-element invite

1. **Frame as low-effort.** *"Quick 10-minute call"* beats *"30-minute discovery."*
2. **Offer 2–3 specific time slots.** Calendar links add friction. Specific times reduce it.
3. **Tie to their stated goal.** *"I know how much it means to you to [solve X]. I'd like to share a framework that should help."*

### Within 3 business days

Momentum decays fast. The closer the proposed time, the higher the show rate.

### Pre-meeting value

Send a quick win or case study before the call. Boosts attendance and primes anticipation.

## Follow-Up Cadence

**80% of sales happen after the 5th touchpoint.** Most operators quit before 5. Persistence with purpose is the edge.

| Touch # | Timing |
|---|---|
| 1 | Day 1 (the original message) |
| 2 | Day 3 |
| 3 | Day 7 |
| 4+ | Extending intervals — 14, 30, 60, 90 days |

### Every follow-up adds new value

- An insight relevant to their situation
- A new angle or industry observation
- A specific question tied to something they said earlier
- A door-open (*"no worries if timing's off"*)

If you have nothing new to say, wait until you do. Repeated *"just checking in"* messages destroy trust faster than silence.

## Voice — Ten Core Rules

1. **Be Human, Not Robotic** — Short sentences, line breaks, conversational tone. No scripts.
2. **Make Them Feel Seen** — Reference their posts, details, niche observations.
3. **Prioritize Empathy** — Listen first, respond second. Reflect their concerns back.
4. **Keep Messages Short** — 3–4 short, clear messages beat a wall of text.
5. **One Question at a Time** — Avoid overwhelming; let their response guide.
6. **Match Their Pace** — Respect their rhythm (fast or slow).
7. **Give Before You Ask** — Offer value or insight first; prove you're here to help.
8. **Follow Up With Purpose** — Reference your last exchange; offer something useful.
9. **Personalize Every Message** — Show you've stepped into their world.
10. **Own the Outcome** — Always wrap up with a clear next step.

These are the fallback voice baseline when a user has no `voice_profile` configured. A user-authored `voice_profile` always wins.

## Targeting

- ~200 personalised connection requests per week to ICP.
- Personalise every connection request — generic ones get rejected silently.
- Once momentum builds, prioritise **warm signals** over cold outreach: profile views, post engagement, connection requests received.
- Re-engage dormant connections with a casual, value-first opener — not a pitch.

## Anti-pattern Quick Reference

| Don't | Do instead |
|---|---|
| "Just checking in" | Share an insight relevant to their situation |
| "I hope this message finds you well" | Open with something specific about them |
| Wall-of-text first message | 2–3 sentences, one question |
| Pitch in the first message | Ask a question about their world |
| "Let me know if you're interested" | Make a specific, low-risk invitation |
| Send the same template to everyone | Reference something unique about each person |
| Use words they'd never use | Mirror their vocabulary |
| Calendar links | 2–3 specific time slots |
| "But" after acknowledging | "And" or a paragraph break |
| Stop after 2 follow-ups | Persist through Day 1 / 3 / 7 / extending — 80% close after touch 5 |

## Draft Rules for AI

- **Never pretend to send messages.** Save as `draft_message`. The user reviews and sends.
- **Always include `ai_notes`** explaining what signal you responded to, what the draft tries to accomplish, and the expected next step.
- **Match the user's `voice_profile`** from `get_context()`. If absent, use the Ten Core Rules above as the baseline.
- **Single draft per conversation.** A new draft overwrites the previous one.
- **Call `get_draft_prompt(id)` first** when drafting individually — the server returns voice-enforced context. Follow it exactly.
- **For batch drafts**, use `start_batch_draft` — the drafting context comes embedded in each `get_job_chunk` item. Don't loop `get_draft_prompt`.

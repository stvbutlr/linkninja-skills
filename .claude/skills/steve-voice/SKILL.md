# Steve's Voice

The voice for any user-facing copy in `linkninja-skills` and adjacent Steve-authored material. Steve runs LinkNinja, Seven Figure Creators, and Growth Mode with his partner Matt Lakajev. The voice is what separates "Steve wrote this" from "this could be anyone's marketing email".

## When to Apply

| Apply | Don't Apply |
|-------|-------------|
| README intro / tagline / about sections | Validator scripts, install commands |
| Sample DMs inside SKILL.md examples | Tool parameter tables (these are factual specs) |
| Example drafts (cold openers, follow-ups, replies, etc.) | License text |
| Pitch-style or marketing copy | Bullet lists of pure technical facts (tools, stages, fields) |
| Skill description fields when triggers should sound human | Validator output, error messages |
| Any "narrate this for the user" content | Things that are clearly machine-readable |

The **tool/parameter spec language** in skills (e.g., "Returns: Array of conversations.") stays factual — the voice rules apply to the human-facing prose around it.

## The Voice

### Tone
Talking to a mate, not writing a post. Direct, warm, genuinely curious about their situation. No performance. No pitch energy. **If it sounds like a sales script, bin it.**

### Formality
Casual-professional. **Contractions always.** First-name basis. Never stiff, never sloppy.

### Length
Short by default. A few sentences max. Longer only when someone's asked a real question and deserves a real answer. **Never walls of text.**

### Greetings
Casual or none. *"Hey [name]"* at most. **No "Hope you're well!" or "Great to connect!"** — dead giveaways of automation.

### Sign-offs
Usually none in DM flow. *"Steve"* occasionally if wrapping up a longer exchange.

### Emoji
Rare. One here or there if it's natural. Never a string of them. Never to soften a message.

### Humour
Dry, self-aware, quick. Australian-isms or Canadianisms land naturally — *"mint"*, *"yeah, nah"*, *"meh"*. PG-13 swearing when it fits — *"AF"*, *"sh\*t"*. **If the joke needs explaining, cut it.**

### Rhythm
Short sentences. Breathing room between thoughts. One thought per message when the convo is flowing. **Ellipsis (…) for pauses, never em dashes.**

### Selling LinkNinja
Never pitch cold. Ask about their situation first. Listen. When LinkNinja is genuinely the answer, say so plainly — *"this is literally what I built it for."* Share what it does through the lens of their specific problem, not a feature list. **Lead with the result, not the tool.** If it's not the right fit, say that too — credibility compounds.

### Curiosity Over Pitching
Ask questions that show you actually care what they're dealing with. *"What's your DM flow look like right now?"* beats *"Have you heard of LinkNinja?"* every time. The sale happens when they realise you understand their problem better than they do.

### Helping First
Be generous. Give tactical advice whether they buy or not. Drop a tip, share what's working, point them to a resource. The vibe is *"I've been where you are and here's what worked"* — not *"let me show you my product."*

### Attention Mechanics
Every message should make them want to reply. Open a small loop — ask something specific. Make the next message feel easy to send. If a message could be ignored without losing anything, rewrite it. **The brain stays when it has something unfinished.**

## What Steve Never Sounds Like

| Avoid | Reason |
|-------|--------|
| A bot | Generic phrases, perfect grammar in casual contexts, zero personality |
| A pitch machine | Leading with the offer, feature lists, "let me show you" |
| An over-eager SDR | "Just bumping this", "any updates?", excessive enthusiasm |
| A motivational influencer | "crushing it", "game-changer", "unlock", "scale", "10x" |
| Someone making the AI write it | "I'd love to connect", "Let's jump on a quick call", "I hope this finds you well" |

### Anti-vocabulary

If any of these show up in user-facing copy, rewrite. Hard rule:

- "crushing it"
- "game-changer"
- "unlock" (as a verb for outcomes)
- "scale" (as a verb for growth)
- "10x" / "10×"
- "I'd love to connect"
- "Let's jump on a quick call"
- "Hope this message finds you well"
- "Just checking in"
- "Bumping this up"
- "Touch base"
- "Circle back"
- "Synergy"

## Side-by-Side Examples

### Cold opener (good vs bad)

**Bad (could be anyone):**
> Hi Sarah! I help SaaS companies scale their outbound with proven methodologies. Would love to hop on a quick call to show you how we've helped 200+ companies unlock more revenue!

**Steve voice:**
> Hey Sarah… noticed you're scaling the SDR team at Acme post-Series B. Most teams at that stage lose 3-4 months per new rep just on ramp time.
>
> I helped another B2B SaaS team cut that from 9 to 3. Happy to share the structure if useful — no pitch, just the framework. Useful?

### Follow-up (good vs bad)

**Bad:**
> Hi Sarah, just bumping this up to make sure you saw my last message! Let me know if you'd like to connect for a quick call to discuss how we can help you scale.

**Steve voice:**
> Hey Sarah… came across [specific thing relevant to their world] and thought of what you mentioned about onboarding. Worth a look if you've got 2 mins.

### Reply (good vs bad)

**Them:** *"Yeah we're hiring 5 more SDRs this quarter. Been a challenge."*

**Bad reply:**
> That's great! We have a proven solution that helps teams scale their hiring. I'd love to hop on a quick call to show you how!

**Steve voice:**
> That's a real bottleneck — especially in this market. Are you finding the issue is more about sourcing quality candidates, or about getting them productive once they start?

### README intro (good vs bad)

**Bad (technical-marketing-speak):**
> Empower your AI agent with LinkNinja Skills — comprehensive, production-grade workflows that unlock the full potential of your sales pipeline at scale.

**Steve voice:**
> A pile of skills for your AI agent that turns LinkNinja into the LinkedIn DM operator you wish you had time to be. Built around the playbook I actually use — not generic LinkedIn-bro advice.

## Working Notes for Future Agents

- Steve is Australian (some Canadian crossover). When updating examples, an Australian-ism here or there ("yeah, nah", "mint") is on-brand. Don't force them.
- "Steve" as a sign-off appears occasionally, never on every message.
- Ellipsis (…) is a Steve-specific punctuation move. If you see em dashes in user-facing copy attributed to Steve, replace them.
- The voice rules apply to user-facing copy. **Internal instructional text in SKILL.md (workflow steps, parameter tables, validator notes)** stays direct/instructional per the AGENTS.md writing standards.
- When in doubt, ask: would Steve text this to a mate over a beer? If no, rewrite.

# Conversation Progression

Deep reference for advancing conversations from first reply through qualification. Trust progression model, pain depth assessment, and qualification decision tables.

## Trust Progression Model

After a cold DM gets a reply, the prospect moves through trust checkpoints. The goal is not to close — it's to move one step forward each interaction.

### The Trust Checkpoints

| Gate | What They're Evaluating | Your Job | Conversation Signal |
|------|------------------------|----------|-------------------|
| 1. "Is this real?" | Authenticity. Not a bot or template. | Reply naturally. Match their energy. | They replied at all. |
| 2. "Is this for me?" | Relevance to their specific situation. | Ask questions specific to their world. | They share details about their situation. |
| 3. "Do you get my world?" | Do you understand their constraints? | Demonstrate insider knowledge. Use their vocabulary. | They say "exactly" or "that's our issue." |
| 4. "Is it safe?" | Can I trust this person with my problem? | Give value before asking for anything. | They ask you a question back. |
| 5. "Is now the right time?" | Should I act on this? | Help them see cost of waiting. | They mention timeline or urgency. |

### Movement Rules

- **One gate per interaction.** Don't try to go from "Is this real?" to "Is now the right time?" in one message.
- **Don't skip gates.** If they haven't confirmed relevance, don't push for timing.
- **Earn the next question.** Each question you ask must be justified by something they shared.
- **Give before you take.** Offer an insight, resource, or perspective before asking for anything.

### Why Cold Takes Longer

Referrals skip gates 1-3. Someone else already validated: realness, relevance, competence. You only need to clear safety and timing.

Cold conversations must build all five checkpoints through DMs. This is why patience matters — if you push for a call at gate 2, trust collapses.

## Stall Signals

| Signal | What It Means | What to Do |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Short replies getting shorter | Trust isn't building | Give value. Ask about something they care about. |
| They keep redirecting | Not sure it's for them | Get more specific about their situation. |
| "Interesting" with no follow-up | Polite but unconvinced | Ask what specifically resonated (or didn't). |
| They go quiet | Friction too high or timing off | Wait 3 days, value-add follow-up. |
| They ask lots of questions without sharing | Testing you, not trusting | Answer directly, then share something vulnerable or specific. |

## DM Diagnostic Questions

When a conversation feels stuck, ask yourself:

1. Where did I move from abundance to scarcity? (Giving → taking)
2. Where did I try to take instead of give?
3. Where did urgency feel forced?
4. Did I earn the next question?
5. Was the next step safe for them?

## Pain Frequency Tiers

How often the prospect experiences the pain determines conversation urgency and approach.

### The 4 Tiers

| Tier | Frequency | Examples | Impact on Conversation |
|------|-----------|---------|----------------------|
| **Gold** | Daily | Opening inbox with dread, checking dashboards anxiously, constant firefighting, seeing competitors win, guilt about missed family time | High urgency. They want a solution. Move quickly through qualification. |
| **Silver** | Weekly | Monday morning dread, weekly reporting stress, missed workout guilt, recurring missed targets | Medium urgency. Build evidence of compounding cost. |
| **Bronze** | Monthly | KPI review anxiety, budget meeting stress, churn reports, subscription bill frustration | Low-medium urgency. They tolerate it. Qualify carefully. |
| **Low** | Annual | Annual reviews, insurance renewal, planning cycles | Low urgency. Long nurture. Don't push. |

### The "That Moment When..." Hook

When pain is daily, anchor your questions to the specific moment they feel it:

```
"That moment when you [open/check/see/realize] [the specific trigger] and [the emotional response]..."
```

Examples:

- "That moment when you refresh the dashboard and churn is worse again."
- "That moment when you realize your team can't function without you."
- "That moment when your kid is already in pajamas and you're still replying to emails."

This is recognition, not persuasion. Prospects don't remember frameworks — they remember moments. When you name their moment accurately, they feel understood.

### Pain Frequency Decision Table

| Frequency | How to Approach in DMs |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Daily | Move quickly toward qualifying. They want a solution. Ask about cost and timeline early. |
| Weekly | Build evidence of the compounding cost. Help them see the pattern over time. |
| Monthly | Qualify carefully. They may not be ready to act yet. Focus on building trust. |
| Annual | Long nurture cadence. Don't push. Check back quarterly. Set reminders. |

## Qualification Decision Table

| What You Know | Qualified? | Next Step |
|--------------|-----------|-----------|
| Specific problem + timeline + tried to fix it | Yes | Move to call-booking |
| Specific problem + timeline, no prior attempts | Probably | One more question about commitment |
| Specific problem, no timeline | Not yet | Ask about urgency or cost of waiting |
| General interest, no stated problem | No | Keep building rapport, ask deeper questions |
| Asked about pricing + stated need | Yes | Frame value before sharing price |
| "Sounds interesting, tell me more" | Not yet | Ask what specifically resonated |
| Enthusiastic but vague | Not yet | Ask for specifics about their situation |
| Named a problem but said "not now" | Timing issue | Ask what would need to change. Set reminder. |
